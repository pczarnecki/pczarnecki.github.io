Paulina Czarnecki

I'm currently a fifth year PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at Columbia University working with Robert Pincus and Lorenzo Polvani on problems in atmospheric science. My research focuses on atmospheric radiation: how we compute it for weather and climate modelling and how we understand its role in the climate system.

Before beginning my doctoral studies, I completed my bachelor of science in Honors Mathematics at the University of Michigan in 2020. While there I worked mainly on problems in computational biology.
